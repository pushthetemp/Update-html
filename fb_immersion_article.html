This is a quick one, why would I want to waste your time?
Facebook immersion, what is that? In this particular case it's referring to the platform's 360 tools. It along with YouTube are the best so far in terms of being able to showcase/support VR content.

360 photos are awesome, especially when it comes to taking in much more of the environment you would not normally get with your stand alone images. Further, if you're in the hospitality or service business you could too benefit from such a feature, whereby you can show a room/location in its entirety and hopefully making it easier to seal the deal- if you have a deal to seal that is.

The bottom line is the fact you able to provide much more visual information, you bring your audience that little bit closer to you, not to mention people will most likely spend much longer on your post because of this immersive experience- especially if you were to start having fun with it by hiding things in the scene for people to look around and find- I mean come on, where's wally has just got a whole heap more interesting, right???

That right there is a solid idea, if you're a clothing brand this could be a great fun game- playing a wheres wally-type thing with the 360 camera. You dress a guy in your exclusive gear , or just one flagship item, put them in a crowded location and your audience need to look thought this 360 photo to find said person. Should they find them, you could maybe give them a voucher, or a discount code of the item or the store in whole. There, don't say I never give you anything.

Just a quick thought. What could you bring to 360 content? On professional and/or personal levels.
The main thing is you need to Bring different, Push different. When you change the way you look at things, the things you look at change.
